West Godavari district, including Chagallu along the Godavari Delta, is one of India's largest cacao-growing regions. Its poly-crop, shade-grown farms produce fine-flavour cacao with a distinct terroir, though the region remains under-recognized compared to established origins abroad.

Fine-flavour cacao is cocoa with a complex sensory profile — fruit, floral, herbal, or nutty notes — shaped by genetics, terroir, and careful post-harvest fermentation and drying, as defined by bodies like the International Cocoa Organization. It's distinct from bulk commodity cocoa, which is graded mainly on volume rather than flavour.

AKIRA Cacao Farms is run by Nandigam Surendra, a cacao farmer based in Chagallu, West Godavari, who personally manages selective harvesting, same-day pod breaking, natural fermentation, and drying rather than buying beans on the open market.
